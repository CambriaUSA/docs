## Maintenance

<Accordion title="Do Cambria countertops require care and maintenance?">
  No — Cambria lives up to its care-free promise. Your Cambria surface requires **no sealing, polishing, or reconditioning** of any kind. To clean it, simply wipe with warm water and a soft cotton cloth, with a mild soap added if desired.

  Because Cambria is inherently nonabsorbent, it does not require the periodic sealing that natural stone countertops typically demand. It is stain resistant right out of the box — and stays that way for the life of the surface.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I add polish to make my Cambria shiny?">
  No — and you don't need to. Cambria countertops **never need polishing or sealing**, which means countertop maintenance can be crossed off your to-do list for good. Applying polish or sealants is unnecessary and may dull the surface over time. Stick to warm water and a soft cloth for everyday cleaning.
</Accordion>

## Preventing Damage

<Accordion title="Is Cambria resistant to surface damage?">
  Yes — Cambria is durable and **more resistant to surface damage than other stone surfaces**. That said, no stone is entirely chip-proof or scratch-proof. A few things to keep in mind:

  - **Chips:** Objects hitting edges — particularly at sinks or dishwashers — may cause chipping. Handle heavy cookware and dishware carefully near the edges of your countertop.
  - **Scratches:** Cambria is scratch resistant, but surface markings are more visible on monotone designs than on multicolored surfaces.

  <Tip>
    Always use a **cutting board** when chopping or slicing. Even on scratch-resistant surfaces, using a cutting board protects both your countertop and the edge of your knives.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I put a hot pan directly on Cambria?">
  No. Natural stone surfaces — including quartz — can be damaged by sudden and/or rapid changes in temperature, especially near edges, as well as by direct or sustained heat exposure. **Cambria may not withstand the direct transfer of heat** from:

  - Pots and pans
  - Electric frying pans and griddles
  - Air fryers
  - Slow cookers and roaster ovens
  - Heat lamps

  <Warning>
    Always use a **closed-weave hot pad** or a **solid trivet** (such as a wooden breadboard) between any heat source and your Cambria surface. This applies to all countertop areas — not just high-traffic cooking zones.
  </Warning>

  For more care guidance, visit the full [Product Care page](/care/product-care).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does Cambria stain?">
  No — Cambria's **nonporous and nonabsorbent surface** is resistant to staining and eliminates the need for sealing. Unlike natural stone surfaces that can absorb spills and harbor stains, Cambria won't soak up food or liquids.

  An added benefit: Cambria's nonporous surface greatly **reduces the potential for bacterial growth** from food absorbing into your countertop — a meaningful advantage in kitchens and food prep areas.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I remove residue or spills from Cambria?">
  Cleaning up spills and residue is simple. For everyday messes:

  1. Wipe the surface with **warm water** and a **soft cotton cloth**
  2. Add a small amount of **mild soap** if desired
  3. Rinse and dry with a clean cloth

  Most residues — including cooking oils, sauces, and food prep mess — clean right up with this method. For stubborn dried-on residue, a small amount of non-abrasive cleaner may be used; consult the [Product Care page](/care/product-care) for approved cleaning products.
</Accordion>

## Food Safety

<Accordion title="Is Cambria safe for food preparation?">
  Yes. Cambria is **NSF-51 certified** for food and splash zones in kitchens. NSF International is The Public Health and Safety Company™, and NSF-51 certification confirms that a surface material is safe for contact with food and food-preparation areas.

  Combined with Cambria's nonabsorbent surface — which does not harbor bacteria from absorbed food particles — Cambria is one of the most hygienic countertop options available for home and commercial kitchens alike.
</Accordion>
