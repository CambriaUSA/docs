Understanding what to expect during the Cambria installation process helps your project go smoothly and eliminates surprises on installation day. Whether you're in the early planning stages or your installation date is just around the corner, these answers cover the most common questions homeowners have before and during a Cambria installation. For questions not addressed here, reach out to your fabricator or installer, or contact **Cambria Customer Care** at 1-866-CAMBRIA (1-866-226-2742).

## Before Your Cambria Installation

<Accordion title="When my old countertop is removed, will I be without a sink?">
  Yes — when your existing countertop is removed, you will temporarily be without a functional sink. However, you can often arrange to have a **temporary sink** installed in the interim. Ask your kitchen and bath dealer or your contractor about temporary sink options well before your installation date so you can plan accordingly.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Who removes my old countertop?">
  Unless it is specifically prearranged and included in your project contract, Cambria's installers will **not** tear out your existing countertop. It is your responsibility to arrange for the removal of your current countertops prior to installation day.

  <Warning>
    Make sure all demolition and removal responsibilities are clearly defined and documented in your purchase agreement before work begins. Don't assume removal is included — confirm it in writing.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I prepare for installation day?">
  The best way to prepare is to review the [Cambria Installation Day Checklist](https://cambriausa.com/content/dam/cusa/sales-marketing-collateral/sell-sheets/cambria-installation-checklist.pdf) well in advance. In general, you should:

  - **Declutter your kitchen** — clear countertops, remove items from cabinets below the counters, and clear floor space along the installation path
  - **Clear access routes** — remove rugs, fragile items, and furniture from areas the crew will use to carry slabs
  - **Confirm coordination** — ensure your plumber and any other trades are aligned on timing with your installer
  - **Have sink and appliance information ready** — model numbers or the fixtures themselves should be on-site for cutout sizing
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How should I prepare walls and cabinets?">
  Your walls and cabinets must be in good condition before Cambria is installed. Specifically, they should be:

  - **Structurally sound** — no soft spots, loose fasteners, or unstable sections
  - **Straight, level, and square** — deviations can cause poor seam fit, inconsistent overhangs, and installation difficulties

  <Note>
    Lack of proper wall and cabinet preparation is one of the most common causes of installation complications. Address any issues before your installation date to avoid delays or additional costs.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if my fixtures and appliances aren't on-site yet?">
  For the most accurate installation, your sink, faucet fixtures, and appliances should be **physically on-site** during the final measurement appointment so your installer can verify exact cutout dimensions. If they cannot be there, provide the installer with the **make and model numbers** of each fixture so they can reference the manufacturer's cutout specifications.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What kind of sink can I use with Cambria?">
  Cambria works with virtually any sink style. Compatible options include:

  - Stainless steel (undermount or drop-in)
  - Cast iron
  - Porcelain
  - Solid surface
  - Composite
  - [Farmhouse-style apron-front sinks](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/farmhouse-sinks-kitchen-countertops)

  Discuss your sink choice with your fabricator early in the process to ensure the correct cutout is planned for your specific model.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Who is responsible for making sure all built-in elements fit together?">
  Responsibility for coordinating how your sink, stove, appliances, cabinets, faucet holes, and other built-in elements fit together **should be clearly defined in your purchase agreement**. This is typically the responsibility of the homeowner, general contractor, or Cambria retailer. If it is not specified, your installer's standard practices will apply.

  <Tip>
    Before signing your purchase agreement, make sure it explicitly identifies who is responsible for fit and coordination of all built-in elements. Resolving this in advance prevents costly disputes after installation.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Will my slab look exactly like my sample?">
  No — the design sample you received is a small, select cut from a single slab and does not fully represent all the design characteristics of the final installed product. Variations in veining, movement, and shading are inherent to quartz surfaces.

  We strongly recommend visiting a [local gallery or slab showroom](https://www.cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/galleries-showrooms) to view a full-size slab before your installation is finalized. Seeing the complete slab gives you a much more accurate sense of how your finished surface will look in your space.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What paint colors go well with my Cambria design?">
  Every Cambria design features a **unique moodboard** showcasing expertly matched paint colors and hardware inspiration tailored to that specific design. You can also use the [Cambria paint match tool](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/planning/tools/paint-match) to receive suggested **Benjamin Moore paint colors** — available exclusively at locally owned paint, decorating, and hardware retailers.
</Accordion>

## During Your Cambria Installation

<Accordion title="Will my walls be protected during installation?">
  Every effort is made to protect your walls and adjacent surfaces during the installation process. However, when a heavy quartz countertop is maneuvered into place, **some scuffing to walls or adjacent fixtures may occur**. Any necessary touch-ups to paint or adjacent surfaces are the responsibility of the homeowner.

  <Info>
    To minimize risk, you may want to take photos of your walls and adjacent surfaces before installation day. This makes it easier to identify and address any scuffs or marks afterward.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do fixtures and appliances need to be on-site during installation?">
  Yes. For proper cutout size verification, your sink, faucet fixtures, and appliances must be **physically available on-site** at the time of installation. Your installer needs these items present to confirm that all cutouts are sized correctly before finalizing the placement of your countertop slabs.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I use Cambria for a DIY installation?">
  Cambria recommends **professional installation** through its network of certified fabricators and installers. Quartz slabs are heavy, precision-cut, and require specialized tools and expertise to fabricate and install correctly. Working with an experienced Cambria-certified professional ensures your investment is protected and your installation meets the standards required by the Full Lifetime Warranty.

  Use the [retailer locator](https://www.cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/locations) to find a certified installer near you.
</Accordion>
