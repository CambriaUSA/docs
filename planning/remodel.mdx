A full remodel is your chance to reimagine your entire space — from layout to surfaces to lighting. It's the most involved of Cambria's three project types, and the most rewarding. When you're redesigning from floor to ceiling, every decision builds on the last, and the countertop you choose sets the tone for everything else in the room. Cambria natural quartz gives you a stunning, durable anchor for that vision — and a team of partner professionals to help you bring it to life beautifully and on budget.

## Planning Your Remodel

Designing your dream kitchen or bath is exciting, especially when you arrive at the process prepared. Here's what to think through before your first professional consultation.

### Establish a Realistic Budget

In a full remodel, budget shapes every decision — from appliances to cabinetry to flooring to countertops. If you're not sure where to start, a professional kitchen and bath planning partner can walk you through how to allocate your spend for maximum impact. Knowing your budget before your first meeting helps your professional recommend Cambria designs and configurations that fit your project without surprises.

### Determine Your Lifestyle Needs

What do you actually want this space to do? An entertaining kitchen has different priorities than a family-first workspace. A spa-inspired bath requires different choices than a high-efficiency morning routine. Thinking through how you live in your home — and how you want to — helps your designer make smarter recommendations. If you're not sure what's possible, an interior designer can open up options you haven't considered.

### Consider Your Timeline

A full remodel involves ordering and coordinating multiple materials — cabinetry, appliances, tile, countertops — and each has its own lead time. If you have a hard target date (a home sale, a renovation reveal, a life event), communicate that upfront. Your professional partners will help you understand what needs to be ordered and delivered before countertop installation can begin, and build a realistic schedule around your goals.

### Explore Cambria Designs and Build a Mood Board

The design exploration phase is the fun part. Take photos of your current space, pull paint colors and flooring samples you're drawn to, and start collecting images from online sources or magazines that capture your vision. Cambria's design detail pages feature expert-curated mood boards showing which paint colors, cabinet finishes, fixtures, and accents pair best with each quartz design — a great starting point for building your own.

<Tip>
  Let your countertop selection lead the design conversation. Choose your Cambria surface first, then build your backsplash tile, cabinet finish, hardware, and paint palette around it. When the most prominent surface in the room sets the direction, everything else falls into place.
</Tip>

## Choosing Your Cambria Surfaces

In a full remodel, Cambria quartz can do more than just countertops. Once you've selected your kitchen or bath surface, consider extending the design to:

- **Backsplash** — Cambria slab backsplashes create a seamless, dramatic look behind ranges and sinks
- **Shower walls and surrounds** — natural quartz is non-porous and easy to clean, making it ideal for wet areas
- **Fireplace surrounds** — a striking surface choice that brings living spaces into the design conversation
- **Islands and bars** — a waterfall edge island in your chosen Cambria design becomes the centerpiece of any kitchen

## The Installation Process

Your Cambria quartz surfaces will be installed by professionals from Cambria's trusted network of fabricators and installers. In a full remodel context, your countertop installation happens after cabinets, appliances, and other structural elements are in place.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Measurement">
    Once your cabinets are set, a professional from your partner's team visits your space to take an official measurement. Final details — edge profiles, seam locations, cutouts for sinks, cooktops, and fixtures — are confirmed at this appointment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customization">
    Your Cambria slabs are reviewed and cut by trained fabrication partners according to your approved specifications. Edge profiles are shaped and all cutouts are precisely made. Your professional will keep you updated on timing throughout fabrication.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Installation">
    On your scheduled installation day, you'll receive instructions for preparing the space. Plan to be home for the appointment. Cambria's installation professionals manage delivery, placement, and finishing — and will walk you through care guidance before they leave.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  To begin the installation process, you'll need to work with a Cambria retail partner. [Schedule a complimentary consultation](https://www.cambriausa.com/consultation) to connect with a professional in your area.
</Note>

## Expert Tips for Remodeling

Remodeling a kitchen or bath is a significant undertaking. These tips — drawn from real project experience — help make it smoother for your whole household.

<Tip>
  **Share your style generously.** Your design partners want to know what inspires you. Bring images from online sources or magazines, paint swatches, flooring samples, and photos of fixtures you've already purchased. The more visual context you provide, the better your professional can match your Cambria selection to the full vision.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Prepare your household for disruption.** A full remodel affects daily life. Cover floors and seal off the work area with plastic sheeting or heavy-duty paper — and plan for a temporary door opening. If the kitchen is out of commission for an extended period, set up a temporary cooking space so family meals don't stop. Communicate the timeline and scope with everyone in the home so expectations are realistic.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Enjoy the moment, not just the result.** Look at progress photos along the way. Celebrate small wins. Have family members sign their names on walls or subfloors before they're closed up. Slow down before adding décor — live in the space first. The work is absolutely worth it.
</Tip>
