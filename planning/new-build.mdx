Building a new home gives you total creative freedom — and Cambria quartz lets you design surfaces that will define every room for generations. Unlike a replacement or remodel, a new build means you're making these decisions before the walls are even up. That's an extraordinary opportunity. Every countertop, island, shower wall, and surface can be considered together, flowing from one inspired choice through the entire home. Cambria is here for all of it — from your first mood board to the moment your surfaces are installed and warranted for life.

## Planning Your New Build

Adding Cambria to a new construction project is straightforward when you approach it with the right preparation. Here's how to set yourself up for success.

### Pick Your Partner

The beauty of a new build is that you don't have to design it yourself. Your builder and professional designers are your most valuable resource — they've guided projects like yours before, they know what works, and they'll help keep your project on time and on budget. Trust them to inspire you and inform you along the way. That's exactly why you hired them.

<Info>
  Beyond your builder, Cambria partner professionals — including kitchen and bath designers and showroom consultants — can help you visualize what's possible and ensure your surface selections come together beautifully across the whole home.
</Info>

### Visualize Your New Home

Before you get into specifics, take time to picture the life you'll live in this house. Who is there? How do the spaces feel? Where do you spend your time? What do you love about it? These aren't abstract questions — your answers directly shape which rooms get the most design investment, which Cambria designs will feel right, and what finishes will stand the test of daily life. There's no need to rush when building your dream home.

### Create Your Space With Intention

A countertop is more than a surface. It's the most-touched, most-seen element in a kitchen or bath — and in a new build, you have the chance to choose it with full intention rather than working around what's already there. Be selective and confident with your selection so you'll love it for life. Consider how each surface will be used, who will use it, and what design legacy you want it to carry throughout the home.

### Explore Cambria Designs

When dreaming, dream big. Browse Cambria's full design palette online, and look for inspiring paint colors, flooring, cabinet styles, and hardware that work alongside your surface selection. Cambria's design detail pages feature expert-curated mood boards showing the specific paints, faucets, and cabinet finishes that pair best with each quartz design — an invaluable tool when you're making holistic decisions across a new home.

<Tip>
  Start your design exploration in the kitchen. Your kitchen countertop choice can influence decisions throughout the entire home — from backsplash tiles and cabinet finishes to shower walls and fireplace surrounds. Begin there, then follow the design thread from room to room.
</Tip>

## How New Build Installation Works

New construction follows a specific sequence that's different from replacement and remodel projects. Understanding the flow helps you plan ahead and avoid delays.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Builder Coordinates the Timeline">
    Your builder manages the overall construction schedule and coordinates with your Cambria partner professional to ensure countertop installation is properly sequenced. Share your Cambria design selections with your builder and professional early so everyone is aligned on timing and ordering.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cabinets and Appliances Are Installed First">
    Because countertops are placed on top of existing structures, **all cabinets, appliances, and other base structures must be fully installed before your Cambria measurement appointment can occur**. Scheduling the measurement too early is the most common cause of delays in new build installations — communicate this requirement clearly with your builder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Measurement Appointment">
    Once cabinets are set, a professional from your partner's team visits the site to take an official measurement of your countertop spaces. Final details — edge profiles, seam placement, cutouts for sinks, cooktops, and fixtures — are confirmed at this appointment. If you've selected a new sink or faucet, have those items on-site.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fabrication and Installation">
    Your Cambria slabs are reviewed and cut by trained fabrication partners to the exact specifications of your space. Once fabrication is complete, your professional team delivers and installs your surfaces. Plan to be on-site for the installation appointment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  All cabinets and appliances must be fully installed before the Cambria measurement appointment can take place. Attempting to measure before base structures are set will result in inaccurate templates and installation delays. Confirm this sequencing with your builder before scheduling.
</Warning>

<Note>
  To begin the installation process, you'll need to work with a Cambria retail partner. [Schedule a complimentary consultation](https://www.cambriausa.com/consultation) to connect with a professional in your area.
</Note>

## Take Our Advice

These tips come from Cambria's experience with thousands of new construction projects. Follow them and your installation will go as smoothly as possible.

<Tip>
  **Start inspired.** Share photos of spaces you love — and samples of items you've already selected — with your designer or Cambria Gallery or Showroom consultant early in the process. The more context your professional has, the more precisely they can match your Cambria selection to the full vision of your home.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Follow the flow.** Your kitchen countertops can set the design direction for the entire home. Once you've chosen your kitchen Cambria surface, let that selection influence your backsplash tile, cabinet finishes, shower walls, and fireplace surrounds. Add hardware and lighting next, and choose paint last — after the permanent surfaces are in place.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Prepare for install.** Confirm with your builder that all cabinets and appliances are fully installed before your Cambria measurement appointment is scheduled. This single step prevents the most common delays in new construction countertop installations.
</Tip>
