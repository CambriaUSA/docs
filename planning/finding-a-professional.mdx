## Why Visiting a Showroom Makes a Difference

A small sample chip tells you about a design's color direction — it can't tell you how the full slab will read across a 10-foot kitchen counter. Visiting a showroom gives you a fundamentally better picture of what your finished space will look like.

**What you get from a showroom visit:**

- **Full-size slabs** — see the real scale of veining, movement, and pattern before you commit
- **Knowledgeable staff** — get honest design guidance from people who work with Cambria every day
- **Design planning support** — bring paint swatches, flooring samples, cabinet door samples, or photos of your space for side-by-side comparisons
- **Project planning meetings** — many showrooms welcome you to bring your designer, contractor, or builder to align on details
- **Referrals to trusted professionals** — showroom staff can connect you with fabricators and installers they work with regularly

<Tip>
  Most Cambria showrooms are walk-in friendly, but calling ahead is always a good idea — especially if you want to meet with a specific design consultant or have a larger planning session. A quick call ensures a staff member is available to give you dedicated time and attention.
</Tip>

## Types of Professionals You'll Work With

A Cambria project typically involves a few different professionals, each playing a specific role:

### Kitchen & Bath Dealers
Your first point of contact in most projects. Dealers help you navigate design selection, surface options, finishes, and edge profiles — and connect you with fabricators and installers. Many also handle full project coordination.

### Fabricators
Fabricators take your measurements and the design you've selected and turn them into precisely cut countertop pieces. They shape the slab, create your edge profile, and make cutouts for sinks, faucets, and cooktops. Cambria-approved fabricators have been trained to work with our specific materials and standards.

### Installers
Professional installers place your finished countertop pieces and ensure they're level, secure, and properly fitted. Installation is never a DIY job with Cambria — it requires specialized equipment and technique.

### Builders
If you're working on new construction, your builder coordinates the Cambria selection and installation process as part of the broader build. Many builders work directly with Cambria partner fabricators and can guide you to design selections that fit the project scope and budget.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I buy Cambria directly from Cambria?">
    No — Cambria surfaces are sold exclusively through authorized partner dealers, select The Home Depot locations, and Cambria Galleries and slab showrooms. You can use the [dealer locator](https://www.cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/locations) to find a location near you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need an appointment to visit a showroom?">
    Most showrooms welcome walk-ins. That said, calling ahead is recommended — especially if you want dedicated time with a design consultant or plan to bring your full project team. It ensures the right staff member is available when you arrive.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What should I bring to a showroom visit?">
    Bring anything that reflects your space and style: paint color swatches, flooring samples, cabinet door samples, tile chips, or photos of your kitchen or bathroom. The more context you give a design consultant, the better they can guide your selection.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I find a Cambria-approved fabricator?">
    Your dealer or Cambria consultant can refer you to an approved fabricator in your area. You can also use the [fabricator/pro locator](https://www.cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/pro-locations) to search specifically for Cambria-approved fabricators and professional partners near you.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
