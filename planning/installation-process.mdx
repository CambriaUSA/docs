Professional installation is the only way to ensure your Cambria surfaces are measured, cut, and fitted to perfection. Cambria works exclusively with a network of trained fabricators and installers who know our materials inside and out — so from the moment a technician visits your home to the moment your new countertops are in place, every detail is handled with precision. Here's exactly what to expect at each stage.

<Warning>
  Cambria **does not recommend DIY installation**. Quartz countertops require specialized tools, precise measurements, and professional handling. Always use a Cambria-approved fabricator and installer to protect your investment and maintain your Full Lifetime Warranty.
</Warning>

## The 4 Stages of Installation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Measurement">
    A technician visits your home to take precise measurements of your space so your countertops can be custom fabricated to fit perfectly. They'll handle all the technical details — your job is to make sure everything is ready before they arrive.

    **Prepare for your measurement appointment:**

    - Ensure cabinets and all supporting structures are fully installed. If you have questions about support requirements, check with your installation associate beforehand.
    - Clear all items off your existing countertops.
    - Have all new fixtures and appliances on-site — or provide model numbers for anything that can't be there in person — so cutouts and fittings are accurate.
    - Make sure the project decision-maker is present during the visit.

    Following these steps helps prevent delays and keeps your project on schedule.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customization">
    Once measurements are confirmed, a Cambria-approved fabricator builds a custom blueprint to determine the ideal flow and pattern continuity for your space. Using that blueprint, they cut your chosen slab to size, shape the selected edge profile, and make precise cutouts for your sink, faucet, and cooktop as needed.

    Every piece is pre-fitted and inspected before it ever leaves the fabrication shop — so when your surfaces arrive, they're ready to go.

    <Note>
      Your design sample is a small, select cut from a single slab — it does not show all the characteristics of the full installed product. Variations in veining, movement, and pattern across the slab are completely normal and expected with natural quartz. To see a full-size representation before committing, visit a [Cambria Gallery or slab showroom](https://www.cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/galleries-showrooms).
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Installation">
    Your professional partner will contact you to schedule the installation appointment. On the day of install, the Cambria partner installer will professionally place and fit your countertops. Project duration varies, so discuss the estimated timeframe with your partner in advance.

    **Prepare for your installation day:**

    - Confirm with your professional partner that all coordination with the installer and other tradespeople (such as plumbers) is arranged.
    - Arrange for your existing countertops to be removed ahead of time. The installer will not remove old countertops unless this is explicitly included in your purchase agreement.
    - Have sinks, faucet fixtures, and appliances on-site so cutouts and fittings can be verified on the spot.
    - Declutter your space — clear walkways and remove items from counters, floors, and cabinets to give the crew room to work.
    - Have the project decision-maker present throughout the installation.

    > **Note on wall scuffing:** Minor scuffing to walls and adjacent surfaces can occur during countertop installation. Any touch-up painting or wall repairs are the homeowner's responsibility.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Celebrate and Register">
    Your beautiful new Cambria surfaces are in — now it's time to enjoy them. Invite friends and family over to show off your space (and feel free to pour a glass of red wine — your countertops are nonabsorbent).

    **Two important last steps:**

    1. **Share your space.** Tag [@CambriaSurfaces](https://www.instagram.com/cambriasurfaces/) on Instagram and use **#MyCambria** for a chance to be featured in our [#MyCambria Gallery](https://www.cambriausa.com/mycambria-gallery).
    2. **Register your surfaces.** Visit [cambriausa.com/warranty/registration](https://www.cambriausa.com/warranty/registration) to register and activate your **transferable Full Lifetime Warranty** — Cambria's guarantee that your surfaces are backed for life.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Things to Know Before Installation Day

### Cabinets and walls must be ready first
Your walls and cabinets must be structurally sound, straight, level, and square before your measurement appointment. Cambria cannot be installed over an unstable or uneven structure.

### Who removes the old countertops?
Unless old countertop removal is explicitly listed in your purchase agreement, **you are responsible for arranging removal** before the installer arrives. Confirm this with your professional when getting your estimate.

### Cambria requires professional installation
Cambria strongly recommends against DIY installation. Improper installation can damage your surfaces and may void your warranty. Your installer should be a trained Cambria partner.

### Samples don't tell the whole story
A small design sample cannot fully represent an entire slab. Pattern flow, veining intensity, and movement will vary across the full surface — this is a natural characteristic of the material, not a defect.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I track the status of my slab order?">
    Contact your retailer or professional partner directly — they have access to your order details and can provide the most up-to-date status on your slab.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What kind of sink can I use with Cambria?">
    Cambria is compatible with virtually any sink style. You can use any undermount or drop-in sink, including stainless steel, cast iron, porcelain, solid surface, and composite materials. [Farmhouse-style apron sinks](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/farmhouse-sinks-kitchen-countertops) are also fully supported.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I prepare for installation day?">
    Review the Cambria Installation Day Checklist — your retailer or professional partner can provide a copy. Key prep steps include removing items from counters and walkways, confirming plumber coordination, and ensuring the project decision-maker is on-site.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will my countertops look exactly like the sample I received?">
    No — and that's by design. Your sample is a small cut from a single slab and does not represent all the pattern characteristics of the full installed surface. Veining, movement, and color variation are natural properties of quartz. We recommend visiting a Cambria Gallery to view a full-size slab before finalizing your selection.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
