Quartz countertop costs depend on several variables — kitchen size, design selection, edge profiles, finishes, fabrication complexity, and regional pricing all play a role. This guide walks you through typical installed price ranges and the key factors that affect your final estimate, so you can have an informed conversation with your professional before requesting a quote.

<Info>
  All price ranges on this page are **guiding estimates only**. Final project costs will depend on a wide variety of factors specific to your project. There may also be project minimums depending on the professional you work with. Always consult a Cambria-authorized professional for an accurate quote tailored to your space.
</Info>

## Installed Price Ranges by Application

The table below reflects typical installed costs for Cambria quartz surfaces across common kitchen applications. "Installed price" includes the material and professional installation labor.

| Application | Typical Size | Installed Price Range |
|---|---|---|
| Small kitchen countertops | 30–50 sq ft | $2,400–$6,500+ |
| Medium kitchen countertops | 50–100 sq ft | $4,000–$13,500+ |
| Large kitchen countertops | 100–150 sq ft | $8,000–$19,500+ |
| Small kitchen island | 8–12 sq ft | $640–$1,560+ |
| Medium kitchen island | 14–20 sq ft | $1,120–$2,600+ |
| Large kitchen island | 22–32 sq ft | $1,760–$4,160+ |
| Average kitchen backsplash | 20–26 sq ft | $1,600–$3,380+ |

Note that these figures don't include extras like waterfall edges or wall cladding, which add square footage. Depending on your design and kitchen configuration, your fabricator may also need multiple slabs to achieve precise pattern matching — so square footage alone isn't always the best predictor of total material cost.

## Factors That Affect Your Total Cost

### Kitchen Size
The total square footage of surface you plan to cover — perimeter countertops, island(s), backsplash, waterfall edges, and any additional wall cladding — directly drives material and installation costs. The more surface area, the more slabs and fabrication labor required.

### Design Selection
Cambria designs featuring large-scale veining or dramatic movement may require extra slab material to properly align the veining across seams. This enhances the visual continuity and elegance of the finished surface but can add to your total material cost. Your fabricator can advise on how much material a specific design requires for your layout.

### Thickness
Cambria quartz slabs are available in **1cm, 2cm, and 3cm** thicknesses. Thicker slabs (3cm) convey a sense of luxury and accommodate more elaborate edge profiles. Thinner options (1cm and 2cm) offer cost advantages while maintaining Cambria's performance standards and are well-suited for backsplashes, wall cladding, fireplaces, and other vertical applications. Your choice of thickness will affect both material and fabrication costs.

### Edge Profiles
Your [countertop edge profile](/surfaces/thicknesses-edge-profiles) is one of the most visible design details in your kitchen. Simple, straight edges are the most affordable option. More intricate profiles — ogee, dupont, rope, and others that require advanced skill or specialized tooling — cost more. Availability of certain profiles also varies by market, so confirm with your fabricator early in the planning process.

### Finish
Cambria offers four finishes: **Polished**, **Cambria Matte®**, **Cambria Satin™**, and **Cambria Luxe™**. Finish availability varies by design and region. Your professional can confirm pricing differences between finish options for your selected design.

### Fabrication Labor
Fabrication involves cutting and finishing the slab to precisely fit your kitchen layout. Costs vary based on the complexity of your project: the number of seams, the edge profile you choose, sink and faucet cutouts, cooktop cutouts, peninsula shapes, and any specialized techniques like thermoforming or fluting.

### Installation Complexity
Installation costs are influenced by project size, shape, the number of cutouts, regional labor rates, and accessibility of your space. Difficult-to-reach areas and out-of-the-way locations typically add to the total.

### Old Countertop Removal
Not all installers include removal of your existing countertops in their estimate. This is an important cost item to clarify upfront — see the checklist below for the exact question to ask.

### Regional Pricing Differences
Labor and overhead costs vary by geography. Pricing in major metro areas typically runs higher than in smaller markets.

## How Cambria Pricing Works

Cambria does not set retail prices. Pricing is determined by your trade professional — the dealer, fabricator, or contractor you work with. This means your final quote will reflect their specific rates, your project's complexity, and your regional market. In general, Cambria quartz is comparable in price to granite and other natural stone surfaces.

The best way to get an accurate number is to request quotes from one or more Cambria-authorized professionals in your area.

## Questions to Ask Your Professional

Before signing any estimate, use this checklist to make sure you have a complete picture of what's included — and what might affect your final cost.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Experience and track record">
    How long has your company been installing Cambria countertops?
  </Step>
  <Step title="What's included in the estimate">
    Does your estimate include delivery, installation, and removal of my old countertops? What is not included?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Design pricing differences">
    What is the price difference between different Cambria designs? Which designs fit my budget?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edge profile costs">
    Is there a price difference between edge profiles? Which profiles are available in my area?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finish recommendation">
    What finish do you recommend for my kitchen, and is there a price difference?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Installation timeline">
    How long does countertop installation typically take for a project like mine?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Old countertop removal">
    Will you remove and haul away my existing countertops? If so, is that included in the estimate?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contractor coordination">
    Will you coordinate with my plumber, electrician, or other contractors?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Permits">
    Do I need a permit for my countertop installation or kitchen remodel?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Labor warranty">
    Do you offer a warranty on your installation labor, beyond Cambria's product warranty?
  </Step>
</Steps>
