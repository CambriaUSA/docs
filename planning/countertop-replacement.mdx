Replacing your countertops is one of the most impactful changes you can make to a kitchen or bath — and it's the simplest Cambria project type. You don't need a full renovation to get a dramatic result. Swapping worn or dated surfaces for beautiful, durable Cambria natural quartz refreshes the entire feel of a room, boosts your home's value, and gives you a surface that's scratch-resistant, stain-resistant, and backed by an industry-leading Full Lifetime Warranty. Here's everything you need to know to make it happen.

## Before You Meet With a Professional

A little preparation before your consultation makes the entire project smoother — and helps your professional build an accurate budget from the start.

### 1. Measure Your Countertops

Take a rough measurement of your existing countertop surfaces. You don't need precision — just enough to give your professional partner a sense of your total square footage so they can help you build a realistic budget. Not sure how? Our [countertop measurement guide](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-measure-your-kitchen-countertops) walks you through it step by step.

### 2. Take Photos of Your Current Space

Capture your countertops, flooring, paint colors, cabinetry, hardware, and any other details that make up your space. These photos give your design professional full context when recommending Cambria designs that will complement — or beautifully contrast — what's already there.

### 3. Establish Your Timeline

If you're working toward a specific date — a holiday gathering, a home listing, a renovation milestone — note it before your consultation. Share those dates with your professional early so the measurement, fabrication, and installation schedule can be built around your needs.

### 4. Explore Cambria Designs

Browse Cambria's full design palette before your consultation so you can walk in with ideas. Use the online search filters to hone in on colors, movements, and finishes that speak to you. You can also use the room visualizer to see how different Cambria designs look in a space like yours, or visit a Cambria Gallery or Showroom to see full slabs in person.

<Tip>
  Bring photos of your space — countertops, flooring, cabinets, hardware — to your showroom visit or consultation. Your design professional can use them to quickly identify Cambria designs that will work beautifully in your home.
</Tip>

## The Installation Process

Your Cambria quartz surfaces are installed by professionals from Cambria's trusted network of fabricators and installers. Every detail is expertly managed so your replacement project goes smoothly from start to finish.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Measurement">
    A professional from your partner's team visits your home to take an official measurement of your countertop space. This is when final design decisions — edge profiles, seam placement, sink cutouts — are confirmed. **If you're purchasing a new sink or faucet, have those items on-site at this appointment** so your professional can account for them in the template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customization">
    Your Cambria slabs are reviewed and precisely cut by trained fabrication partners. Edge profiles are shaped and all cutouts are made according to your approved specifications. Your professional partner will keep you informed on timing throughout this stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Installation">
    Once an installation date is confirmed, you'll receive instructions for preparing your space. Plan to be home for the full appointment. Your installers will handle delivery, placement, and final detailing — and walk you through care and maintenance before they go.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  To start the installation process, you'll need to work with a Cambria retail partner. [Schedule a complimentary consultation](https://www.cambriausa.com/consultation) to get connected with a professional in your area.
</Note>

## Expert Tips for a Smooth Replacement

These professional-approved tips help you avoid common hiccups and keep your project on track.

<Tip>
  **Plan for countertop removal.** Your existing countertops need to come out before the new ones go in. Removal is your responsibility to arrange unless removal is specified in your purchase agreement — feel free to ask your installer upfront whether they can provide this service.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Have your new sink and faucet on hand for the measurement appointment.** If you're upgrading your sink or faucet at the same time, your professional needs those items present to accurately template the cutout. Don't wait until after measurement to order them.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Declutter before installation day.** Clear all walkways and remove everything from your countertops, the floors around the work area, and any connecting cabinets. The easier you make access, the faster and cleaner your installation will go.
</Tip>
