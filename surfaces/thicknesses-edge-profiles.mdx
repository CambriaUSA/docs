Your countertop is more than a finishing touch — it shapes the entire look and feel of your space. Cambria gives you precise control over two of the most impactful customization choices: slab thickness and edge profile. Whether you want a slim, minimalist surface for a sleek backsplash or a bold, thick countertop with an architectural edge that commands attention, the right combination of thickness and edge profile turns your design vision into a reality.

## Quartz Slab Thicknesses

Cambria quartz is available in [1cm, 2cm, and 3cm thicknesses](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/a-guide-to-countertop-thickness), each suited to different applications and design goals. No thickness is inherently better than another — the right choice depends on where you're installing it and the look you want to achieve.

### 1cm — Lightweight and Versatile

At 1cm, Cambria quartz is lightweight yet strong, making it the ideal choice for vertical applications where a thinner, more refined profile is needed. Common uses include:

- **Backsplashes** — seamless, easy-care surfaces behind cooktops and sinks
- **Shower surrounds and walls** — a spa-quality finish that's nonporous and never needs sealing
- **Wall cladding and feature walls** — dramatic large-format installations without structural concerns
- **Floating shelves** — a modern, minimalist shelf solution with the same durability as a countertop

The lightweight design delivers a clean, minimalist look while offering the same durability, stain resistance, and maintenance-free performance as thicker Cambria slabs.

[View 1cm design palette →](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/quartz-colors/1cm-designs)

### 2cm — Sleek and Modern

At 2cm, Cambria quartz strikes the ideal balance between a slender modern profile and substantial presence. It creates a sophisticated, clean look without the added bulk of 3cm, making it a popular choice for:

- **Countertops** — especially in modern and contemporary kitchen and bath designs
- **Wall cladding** — refined surfaces that add luxury to accent walls and feature panels
- **Floating shelves** — with slightly more visual weight than 1cm for a more grounded appearance

2cm designs offer lasting durability and effortless maintenance — and when paired with a mitered edge, can create the striking visual impression of a much thicker slab.

[View 2cm design palette →](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/quartz-colors/2cm-designs)

### 3cm — Maximum Impact

For bold elegance and maximum visual presence, 3cm is the most popular Cambria thickness for countertops. Its thicker profile provides additional strength, timeless appeal, and — critically — access to the widest range of edge profiles, including the most intricate and architectural options. Best uses include:

- **Kitchen countertops** — where the substantial edge adds presence and a premium feel
- **Bathroom vanities** — where thickness communicates luxury
- **Islands and bars** — where 3cm's weight and presence become the visual centerpiece of the room

## Edge Profiles

Your edge profile is the final frame around your countertop — and it has an outsized effect on the overall feel of the space. Cambria offers 19 distinct edge profiles organized across three style families: Modern, Classic/Transitional, and Architectural.

### Choosing the Right Edge Profile

Before diving into the full list, keep these five factors in mind:

- **Style:** Ornate edges like ogee or half bullnose suit traditional spaces; eased, bevel, or straight edges complement modern designs.
- **Functionality:** Bullnose edges are more family-friendly, reducing sharp corners in high-traffic areas.
- **Thickness compatibility:** Some profiles require 3cm to be properly fabricated. A mitered edge on a thinner slab creates the visual impression of a much heavier countertop.
- **Maintenance:** Simpler edges like eased or bullnose are easier to clean; intricate profiles with more crevices require extra attention.
- **Budget:** Elaborate edges like ogee or waterfall can increase fabrication costs; eased edges offer a sleek, cost-effective option.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do edge profiles affect the overall performance of the countertop?">
    No. Edge profiles are purely an aesthetic decision and have no impact on the durability, stain resistance, or performance of your Cambria countertop. Choose the profile that best suits your style and space.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does countertop thickness affect performance?">
    No. Both 2cm and 3cm thicknesses offer the same exceptional durability, stain resistance, and maintenance-free benefits. Thickness is primarily a visual and application-driven choice — not a performance one. (1cm is recommended for vertical applications rather than horizontal countertop use.)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does thickness affect which edge profiles are available?">
    Thickness directly determines which edge profiles can be fabricated. Some intricate or elaborate profiles — particularly architectural edges like Cornice, Mesa, and Cascade — require additional thickness to be properly constructed. A mitered edge (Rimrock or Idris) can be applied to thinner slabs to create the visual impression of a heavier countertop. Always confirm profile availability with your fabricator based on your chosen thickness.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Are all edge profiles available in all markets?">
    Not all edge profiles are available in every market. Contact your local authorized Cambria dealer or call **1-866-CAMBRIA (1-866-226-2742)** to confirm which profiles are available in your area.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What thickness should I choose for a kitchen countertop?">
    3cm is the most popular choice for kitchen countertops. Its thicker profile delivers a bold, premium presence and gives you access to the widest selection of edge profiles. If you prefer a sleeker, more modern look, 2cm is an excellent alternative with the same durability.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
