While Cambria quartz is exceptionally durable and completely maintenance free, certain chemicals, tools, and habits can cause lasting damage to your surfaces. The good news: protecting your investment takes almost no effort — just a few simple rules to follow. Here's what to avoid, and what to use safely instead.

---

## Quick Reference: Use This, Not That

| ✅ Use This | ❌ Not This |
|---|---|
| Warm water + soft cotton cloth | Abrasive scrub pads or steel wool |
| Mild liquid soap (if desired) | Bleach or bleach-based cleaners |
| Closed-weave hot pad or solid trivet | Placing hot pots or pans directly on the surface |
| Cutting board for food prep | Cutting directly on the quartz surface |
| pH-neutral cleaner (quartz-safe) | Oven cleaners, Comet®, Soft Scrub® |
| Nothing (no sealing needed) | Sealers, penetrants, or topical treatments |

---

## Cleaners to Avoid

<Warning>
  Do not expose your Cambria surface to abrasive, strong alkaline, acid, free radical, or oxidizing cleaners — whether high, neutral, or low pH. Various chemicals are corrosive and/or erosive in their ability to attack any surface, including Cambria quartz.
</Warning>

The following products should never come into contact with your Cambria surface:

### Abrasive and Caustic Cleaners
- **Comet®** and similar powder cleansers
- **Soft Scrub®** and cream-based abrasive cleaners
- **S.O.S.® pads** and steel wool scrubbing pads
- Any product containing **pumice**
- **Oven cleaners** — highly caustic and corrosive

### Bleach and Oxidizing Agents
- **Bleach** and bleach-based cleaning products
- **Tarnish cleaners** and silver polishes
- **Oxidizing cleaners** of any kind

### Chemical and Industrial Products
- **Paint removers** and furniture strippers
- **Battery acid** — even incidental contact from a leaking battery can cause damage
- Any products marketed as **abrasive** or **heavy-duty** surface cleaners

---

## Treatments to Never Apply

Even well-intentioned treatments can permanently damage your Cambria surface's appearance.

<Warning>
  Do not apply sealers, penetrants, or topical treatments to Cambria quartz under any circumstances. These products will wear off unevenly and cause the gloss to appear dull or inconsistent.
</Warning>

Never apply any of the following:

- **Sealers or penetrants** — Cambria's nonporous surface has no need for sealing, and sealers will degrade the finish
- **Polish or wax** — unnecessary and damaging to the surface appearance
- **Any "stone treatment" products** — these are designed for porous natural stones and are not appropriate for quartz

## Habits to Avoid

Beyond cleaning products, a few everyday habits can cause damage over time:

### Heat
Placing hot items directly on quartz is one of the most common causes of surface damage. Natural stone surfaces can be damaged by sudden and/or rapid changes of temperature, especially near edges.

**Always place the following on a trivet or hot pad:**
- Pots and pans fresh from the stove
- Electric frying pans and griddles
- Slow cookers and Instant Pots
- Air fryers
- Roaster ovens
- Heat lamps

### Cutting Directly on the Surface
Using your countertop as a cutting surface — especially for repeated, direct knife contact — can leave visible scratch marks, particularly on monotone designs. A minor slip of the knife will dull the knife and not harm the surface, but habitual direct cutting adds up.

### Impact Near Edges
Edges, particularly those around sinks and dishwashers, are the most vulnerable part of any stone surface. Avoid:

- Dropping heavy objects onto the countertop or edges
- Sharp impacts from pots, pans, or tools striking the edge
- Excessive leverage or pressure on unsupported overhangs

## What to Use Instead

For the vast majority of everyday messes, you don't need anything fancy:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Everyday Cleaning" icon="sparkles">
    Warm water and a soft cotton cloth handles nearly everything. Add a drop of mild liquid dish soap for greasy residues.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stubborn Residues" icon="droplets">
    Gently work with warm water and a soft cloth — most residues release easily. Let warm water sit briefly on dried-on material before wiping.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tougher Spots" icon="shield-check">
    Use a non-abrasive, pH-neutral cleaner that is explicitly approved for use on quartz surfaces. Always rinse thoroughly with warm water afterward.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Heat Protection" icon="flame">
    Keep a closed-weave hot pad or solid trivet on your counter within easy reach. A breadboard doubles as a great trivet for most cooking situations.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Important Reminders

<Note>
  Cambria is not heat-proof, chemical-proof, or fracture-proof. Being aware of these limitations — and taking simple precautions — is all you need to keep your surfaces looking flawless for life.
</Note>

- **Heat-proof?** No. Always use a trivet.
- **Chemical-proof?** No. Stick to warm water and mild soap.
- **Fracture-proof?** No. Protect edges from impacts, especially near sinks.

Following these simple guidelines, combined with the effortless daily cleaning routine described in the [Product Care guide](/care/product-care), is all it takes to enjoy your Cambria surfaces for a lifetime — backed by the industry's most comprehensive warranty.
