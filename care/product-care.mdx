Cambria quartz is the definition of low-maintenance luxury. Your surfaces never require sealing, polishing, reconditioning, or any topical treatment — ever. All it takes to keep your countertops looking as flawless as the day they were installed is a little warm water and a soft cloth. Here's everything you need to know about caring for your Cambria surfaces.

---

## Daily Cleaning

Cleaning your Cambria quartz couldn't be simpler. For everyday care, all you need is:

- **Warm water**
- **A soft cotton cloth**
- **Mild soap** (optional)

That's it. No specialty cleaners, no stone-specific products, and no scrubbing required. Because Cambria's surface is nonporous and nonabsorbent, spills sit on top rather than soaking in — so a gentle wipe is almost always all it takes.

<Note>
  Always use a cutting board when preparing food. Cutting directly on quartz can leave visible scratch marks, particularly on monotone designs.
</Note>

## You'll Never Need To…

One of the most distinctive things about Cambria quartz is everything you *don't* have to do. Unlike granite, marble, and many other natural stones, your Cambria surfaces will never need:

- **Sealing** — the nonporous surface eliminates the need entirely
- **Polishing** — the finish is permanent and doesn't require upkeep
- **Reconditioning** — no periodic treatments or professional refinishing
- **Any topical treatments** — these actually cause more harm than good (see [What to Avoid](/care/what-to-avoid))

## Heat Precautions

Natural stone surfaces — including quartz — can be damaged by sudden or rapid changes in temperature, especially near edges, as well as by direct and sustained heat on the top surface.

<Warning>
  Do not place hot items directly on your Cambria quartz surface. Always use a closed-weave hot pad or solid trivet.
</Warning>

Use a closed-weave hot pad or solid trivet (such as a breadboard) under all of the following:

- Pots and pans
- Electric frying pans and griddles
- Slow cookers
- Air fryers
- Roaster ovens
- Heat lamps

A well-chosen trivet can complement your kitchen design while protecting your investment — look for a geometric metal trivet for a modern space or a natural wood trivet for a more organic, farmhouse feel.

## Stain Resistance

Cambria quartz is nonporous and nonabsorbent, which means liquids — including wine, juice, oils, and coffee — cannot penetrate the surface. This makes your countertops naturally resistant to staining and greatly reduces the potential for bacterial growth from food absorbing into the surface.

If a spill does occur, clean it up with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap if desired. Most residues come right off without any effort.

---

## Scratch and Chip Resistance

Cambria quartz is durable and more resistant to surface damage than many other stone surfaces. That said, it's important to understand the limits of any natural stone material:

- **No stone is chip-proof.** Objects hitting edges — particularly around sinks and dishwashers — can cause chips over time.
- **No stone is fully scratch-proof.** While everyday knife use will dull your knife rather than harm the surface, visible scratch marks can appear on more monotone designs with repeated direct cutting.
- **Always use a cutting board.** This is the single best habit to protect your surface's appearance over the long term.

## About Cambria Matte® Finish

If you've chosen a Cambria Matte® finish, there are a few additional things to keep in mind:

- The matte surface **will show surface markings** from normal use — fingerprints, smudges, and similar marks are more visible than on a polished finish.
- These markings are **easily removed** with a soft cotton cloth and warm water; use a mild soap if desired.
- Matte finish **does not shine** and is designed to maintain a consistent low-sheen appearance.
- In **high-traffic areas** such as flooring, the matte finish may appear to soil or stain more readily from soiling — this is normal and expected.

## Food Safety

Cambria surfaces are **NSF-51 certified** for food and splash zones in kitchens. This independent certification confirms that your countertop is a safe surface for direct food preparation — no sealing or chemical treatments required to maintain that safety standard. The nonporous surface also means you're not introducing bacteria-harboring crevices into your food prep area.

## Additional Surface Information

A few more things worth knowing about your Cambria surfaces:

- **Natural variation is normal and expected.** Cambria is created from pure natural quartz, and variation in color, pattern, size, shape, and shade are inherent characteristics of the material. Color blotches are intentionally included in many designs. These variations do not affect performance.
- **Samples are small select cuts** from a larger slab and may not fully exhibit all design characteristics of the installed product. This is not a defect.
- **Seams are visible.** Cambria is not a seamless product. Where seams occur, changes in pattern and shade will be visible.
- **Cambria is not a structural support material** and must be supported in every application.
- **Thickness matters for the application:** 2cm and 3cm thicknesses are for countertops and horizontal surfaces; 1cm thickness is for vertical surface finishes such as walls and full-height backsplash only.
