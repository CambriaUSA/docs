Want to know how much quartz countertops cost? Behind the stunning look of [quartz countertops](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/kitchens-baths/kitchen-countertops) is a varied landscape of cost considerations. So, once you’ve entered the world of kitchen design, where every decision counts and every detail matters, knowing the cost factors of quartz countertops will empower you to make informed decisions that can transform your kitchen into a space that reflects your style and meets the needs of your lifestyle.

Among the many selections you’ll make when creating your kitchen [design plan](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/planning/tools/kitchen-bath-planner), countertops will be one of the most impactful in terms of aesthetic beauty and functionality. That’s why quartz countertops, with their unparalleled durability and gorgeous appearance, are an incredibly popular material choice. But how much do quartz countertops cost? Let’s dive in.

## Quartz Countertops: How Quality and Kitchen Size Impact Cost

Keep in mind that not all quartz surfaces are created equal. High-quality quartz countertops, made using advanced technology and a higher concentration of exceptionally pure quartz minerals, typically command a higher price.

[Cambria quartz surfaces](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/why-cambria) are proudly made in America, where superior craftsmanship and innovation drive us to deliver the finest products. Cambria is not only naturally durable and [maintenance free](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/product-care), it also never requires sealing, is built to resist chips, scratches, and stains, and is available in an endless array of beautiful designs and colors. Our [transferable Full Lifetime Warranty](https://www.cambriausa.com/warranty) demonstrates our confidence in the quality of our products.

Quartz quality is important, and is one of several important factors associated with why the average cost of quartz countertops sits within a fairly wide range. For example, let’s begin with kitchen surface size. Although fabrication and installation costs associated with specific projects will vary, it's fairly safe to say that the cost of Cambria quartz countertops can range between $2,400–$6,500+ for a small kitchen project, between $4,000–$13,500+ for a medium-sized kitchen project, and $8,000–$19,500+ for a large kitchen project.

## Quartz Countertop Cost Factors

Next up is a primer on other key factors that will impact the final cost of your quartz countertop purchase. Use this information as part of your [kitchen layout design](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/what-to-consider-in-a-kitchen-island-layout) process since the following decisions will play into the overall design aesthetic you’re trying to achieve in your space.

### Kitchen Size

As noted above, the size of your kitchen and the amount of surface you plan to cover with quartz, including perimeter countertops, backsplash, kitchen island(s), waterfall edges, and any additional [wall cladding](https://www.cambriausa.com/unique-installs/quartz-wall-cladding) will impact the total cost of the quartz surface material in your [project](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/planning). The full project size combined with your desired quartz design will determine how much actual square footage will be needed.

### Quartz Countertop Design Selection

Cambria designs that have [large vein pattern](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/quartz-colors?features=Vein&features=Wave) can require extra slab material to align the veining across any necessary seaming. The visual impact of this continuous veining contributes to the elegance, sophistication, and refinement of your space.

---

### Thickness

Cambria quartz slabs are available in 1cm, 2cm, and 3cm thicknesses. The 1cm option is designed for a wide range of applications—from vertical and weight-sensitive installations like showers, fireplaces, and wall cladding to custom uses including backlighting, bookmatching, and thermoforming. The 1cm and 2cm options may offer cost advantages while maintaining Cambria’s signature durability and style. See how select Cambria designs bring bold ideas to life in our 1cm thickness.

Choosing your countertop slab thickness is a matter of personal preference and design aesthetics as well as budget considerations. Thicker quartz countertops exude a sense of luxury and make a bold statement in kitchen or bathroom design. They can also accommodate decorative [edge profiles](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/thicknesses-edge-profiles), adding intricate detail to the overall look.

Conversely, a thinner quartz countertop can offer a sleek and contemporary appearance ideal for modern or minimalist design schemes. Ultimately, your slab thickness decision will hinge on your individual style preferences, but will also impact the cost of your quartz countertop.

### Edge Profiles

From sleek and simple to ornate and elegant, a [Cambria quartz countertop edge profile](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/thicknesses-edge-profiles) adds dynamic details that significantly impact the overall look and feel of your space. [Selecting the right quartz edge](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-choose-countertop-edge-profile) is crucial to achieving your desired style and functionality—and ensures your countertops not only look stunning but serve your specific needs effectively. Availability of certain edge profiles can vary by market and any edge profile that requires an advanced level of skill or specialized tools to create will be more expensive.

### Countertop Finishes

Cambria offers four [innovative quartz finishes](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/finishes-textures): [polished](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/finishes-textures/polished), [Cambria Matte®](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/finishes-textures/matte), [Cambria Satin™](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/finishes-textures/satin), and [Cambria Luxe™](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/finishes-textures/luxe). Finish availability can vary depending on your region and quartz design selection, although most designs are available in polished and Cambria Matte. Your professional can help determine your quartz countertop cost estimate based on your design and finish preferences.

---

### Fabrication

Fabrication of quartz surfaces involves cutting and finishing the stone to fit your specific kitchen layout. [Expert fabrication](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-add-impact-and-personality-to-your-kitchen-design) of high-quality quartz requires skilled professionals with specialized tools. Your fabricator should be trained in the most up-to-date techniques and be committed to following all rules, regulations, and requirements involved in fabricating, finishing, and installing stone products.

Fabrication labor costs vary depending on the complexity of your project design, the number of seams, and the type of edge profile you choose. Additional fabrication details can include sink and faucet cutouts, peninsulas, stovetop cutouts, etc. Even more specialized fabrication can provide distinctive island shapes, thermoforming, and fluting.

### Installation

Similar to fabrication mentioned above, Cambria quartz countertops are [installed by skilled technicians](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/cambria-quartz-countertop-installation) and installation costs are associated with the complexity of the job. The size and shape of your countertops as well as things like cutouts and cooktops will be taken into account. The location of your project matters too. Out-of-the-way or difficult to access spaces and regional differences in costs to do business will likely bump up your [installation](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/faq/installation) costs.

### Old Countertop Removal

Some, but not all quartz countertop installers, include removal of your old countertops as part of their [installation service](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/cambria-quartz-countertop-installation). Be sure to confirm this detail when obtaining an estimate for your quartz countertops. Also, since countertop removal is a key step in the remodeling process, your installer will need to know for planning purposes if you’re only replacing old countertops or if your countertop removal and installation are part of a larger [remodeling project](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/renovation-checklist).

You can also call us with questions at [1-866-CAMBRIA](https://www.cambriausa.com/tel:1-866-226-2742) (1-866-226-2742).

How long do quartz countertops last?

With proper care, quartz countertops can retain their beauty and functionality for decades, making them a durable and long-lasting investment for any home. Cambria’s quartz surfaces are backed by a [transferable Lifetime Warranty](https://www.cambriausa.com/warranty).

What are quartz countertops made of?

[Quartz](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops) is one of the hardest, most abundant natural elements. At Cambria, we combine high-quality, exceptionally pure quartz with pigments and binder to create durable, compact slabs with unsurpassed performance.

How much maintenance do Cambria quartz countertops require?

None! Cambria quartz countertops are completely maintenance free. They are extremely durable, nonabsorbent, and never need to be sealed. They are [easy to clean](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/product-care), stain resistant, and don't harbor harmful bacteria. But, it’s important to note that all stone countertops, including quartz, can be damaged by sudden temperature changes. That’s why we recommend placing pot holders or closed-weave trivets under anything hot.
