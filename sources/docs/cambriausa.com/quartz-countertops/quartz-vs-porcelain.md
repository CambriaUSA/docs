## Quartz vs. Porcelain

---

Choosing between quartz and porcelain countertops? We’re here to help you find the right fit for your home.

---

### 

What Is Quartz?

Quartz countertops are made of natural quartz crystals combined with binders and pigments to create the final product. This process enables quartz countertops to be extremely durable, nonabsorbent, and resistant to stains, scratches, and chips. Available in a wide range of designs, colors, and finishes, Cambria quartz surfaces also offer rich depth, texture, and versatile edge profiles for a truly luxurious look.

### 

What Is Porcelain?

Porcelain countertops are crafted through a precise manufacturing process. Raw materials are ground into a fine powder, mixed with water, and shaped into durable, nonabsorbent slabs. Decorative patterns are applied to the top of the surface with inkjet technology to create the look of natural stone or other designs, but these designs do not run through the slab. While resistant to stains, scratches, and heat, porcelain may be prone to chips that cannot be repaired.

## Details That Matter

---

There’s a lot to consider when comparing quartz and porcelain. Here’s what to keep in mind.

---

---

### 

Maintenance

Quartz and porcelain countertops are effortless to maintain. Due to being nonabsorbent, both materials are stain resistant and never need sealing. Some options, including Cambria quartz and certain porcelain surfaces, are [NSF-51](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/quartz-is-the-ultimate-food-safe-surface) certified for safe food preparation. To [clean](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-clean-quartz-countertops) either, just wipe with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired.

### 

Durability

Quartz and porcelain are both durable and scratch resistant. Porcelain tends to be a popular choice for outdoor spaces, though prolonged exposure may lead to fading over time. It is also more brittle and prone to chipping, especially along edges or during installation. Unlike porcelain, which is often impossible to fix, quartz chips can typically be repaired. Cambria surfaces also come with a transferable [Full Lifetime Warranty,](https://cambriausa.com/warranty) ensuring long-term durability and repair support, if ever needed.

### 

Appearance

Quartz offers a wide range of colors and patterns, with depth and luxury running through the slab and edges. Some designs feature raised or debossed veining for enhanced dimension and texture. Porcelain’s surface-level inkjet designs can mimic natural stone but has limited edge profile options compared to the more intricate possibilities of quartz. While porcelain’s thin profile provides a sleek look, Cambria’s [1cm quartz surfaces](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/thicknesses-edge-profiles/1cm) offer additional benefits. They are lightweight, making installation a breeze; are incredibly versatile for unique applications like showers and fireplace surrounds; and can even be backlit for dramatic illumination. They’re also available in [multiple finishes](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/finishes-textures) for greater design flexibility.

## Frequently Asked Questions

---

---

Is Cambria resistant to surface damage?

Cambria is durable and more resistant to surface damage than [other stone](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops). However, all stone can be damaged by force and no stone is chip-proof. Objects hitting edges, particularly at sinks or dishwashers, may cause chips. Cambria is scratch resistant; however, no stone surface is scratch-proof. Surface markings are more visible on monotone designs than multicolored surfaces.

How can I purchase Cambria quartz countertops?

Purchasing Cambria is easy. Use our [retailer locator](https://cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/locations) to find a retail location near you that can provide you with an estimate for your countertop needs. Generally, you will need the dimensions of your cabinet layout, information regarding backsplash requirements, edge profile selections, and your sink and stove locations and dimensions to get an accurate estimate.  If you have design questions or need help with recommendations, each of our partners can provide their expertise to step you through.

Is Cambria quartz made or manufactured in a plant?

Our slab manufacturing facility converts natural quartz into slabs by combining quartz with a small amount of pigment and binders. Through the process, we are able to create slabs that are nonabsorbent, durable, and compact.
