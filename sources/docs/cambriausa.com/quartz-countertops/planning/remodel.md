## Planning Your Remodel

---

Designing your dream kitchen or bath is exciting—especially when you’re properly prepared. Here’s what to keep in mind before you begin your project.

### 

Establish a Realistic Budget

In remodeling, budget definitely matters. If you don’t know yours yet, a professional kitchen and bath planning partner can show you the best ways to spend on appliances, [countertops](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-much-do-quartz-countertops-cost), cabinetry, and everything perfect for your project needs.

### 

Determine What You Desire

Want a spa-like bathroom? Kitchen made for entertaining? More storage or better flow in your home? Understanding your lifestyle can help determine your design choices. Not sure what’s possible? An [interior designer](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/working-with-interior-designer) can help.

### 

Consider Your Timeline

Want your remodel done by a specific date? Plan ahead. Key items must be purchased and delivered before work happens. Our professional partners can help you understand what’s needed before your countertop installation. 

### Explore Cambria Designs

Envisioning your future kitchen or bath is the fun part. Take photos of your space and pull inspiring paint colors, flooring, cabinetry, and accents. Or visit our design detail pages to see expert-curated moodboards that highlight the paint colors, fixtures, and accents that go best with each of our quartz designs.

## Expert Tips for a Smooth Project

---

To ensure your project goes as smoothly as possible, start with a few pointers from us.

---

### 

Share Your Style

Your design partners want to know what inspires you. Bring images from online or magazines, samples of items you’ve already purchased, and paint colors and flooring samples you love. If you have white cabinets, remember to bring a paint swatch to make sure they go well with your countertop selection.

### 

Be Prepared

Remodeling can disrupt the family home. Mentally prepare everyone for what’s to come. Cover floors and seal off rooms with plastic sheeting or heavy-duty paper—remember to create a temporary door. If necessary, create a temporary kitchen to not disrupt meals together. For more tips, review our [Kitchen & Bath Design Planner](https://cambriausa.com/content/dam/cusa/sales-marketing-collateral/sell-sheets/kitchen-and-bath-planner.pdf).

### 

Enjoy the Moment

The goal is to have fun throughout the entire remodel process. Look at progress photos. Enjoy the little wins. Have your family sign their name behind walls before the drywall goes on. Slow down and add décor later. Most importantly, remember all this work is absolutely worth it in the end.
