---

There’s no denying it. Quartz is an exceptional material for luxurious surfaces. But why does gray quartz make a standout selection? Choosing gray [quartz countertops](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops) enhances your living area with a captivating, timeless sophistication. Naturally stain resistant, it’s easy to clean and maintain. Just simply wipe it with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. No sealing is necessary. ## Gray Quartz Countertops: What to Consider

---

There’s no denying the presence gray quartz countertops create in a home. If you’re considering them for your new build, remodeling project, or countertop refresh, it’s important to consider the benefits and drawbacks.

Quartz is naturally [maintenance free](https://www.cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-clean-quartz-countertops), which means you’ll never have to spend your free weekends sealing or polishing your surfaces. Extremely durable and resistant to stains and scratches, they’re the perfect surface for high-traffic areas. Like with all natural surfaces, however, Cambria may not withstand the direct transfer of heat from pots and pans and other cooking units such as electric frying pans and griddles, slow cookers, air fryers, roaster ovens, and heat lamps. Therefore, the use of a closed-weave hot pad or solid trivet such as a breadboard is always recommended to prevent heating the product.

In terms of financial investment, quartz can be competitive with or at the upper end versus other surfaces. That said, you could end up spending more time and money creating, [installing](https://www.cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/faq/installation), and maintaining concrete countertops—only to have them not perform as well.

With any dark surface, there is a chance to see marks and fingerprints. Fortunately, gray quartz surfaces require very [simple upkeep](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-clean-quartz-countertops), meaning all you have to do is simply wipe them down with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired, to bring back their beautiful look.

If you are looking to refresh your kitchen without completely overhauling your space, gray quartz countertops are a stylish, neutral option likely to complement your existing furnishings. A bold, gray countertop design could even pull attention away from older cabinets.

Do gray Cambria quartz countertops stain?

No matter the color—[white](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/white-designs), [black](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/black-designs), or gray—the nonporous and nonabsorbent surface of Cambria’s gray quartz countertops are resistant to staining and eliminates the need for any sealing. Its nonabsorbent surface also greatly reduces the potential for bacterial growth from food absorbing into your countertop.

Purchasing Cambria is easy. Use our [retailer locator](https://cambriausa.com/dealer-locator) to find a retail location near you that can provide you with an estimate for your countertop needs. Generally, you will need the dimensions of your cabinet layout, information regarding backsplash requirements, edge profile selections, and your sink and stove locations and dimensions to get an accurate estimate.  If you have design questions or need help with recommendations, each of our partners can provide their expertise to step you through.

Do gray Cambria quartz countertops require care and maintenance?

No, keeping with its [care-free promise](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/product-care), Cambria requires no sealing, polishing, or reconditioning. Simply wipe with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. Due to its nonabsorbent nature, it does NOT require regular sealing and is stain resistant.
