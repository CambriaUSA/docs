## Quartz vs. Quartzite

---

Choosing between quartz and quartzite countertops? We’re here to help you find the right fit for your home.

---

### 

What Is Quartz?

Quartz countertops are made of natural quartz crystals combined with binders and pigments to create the final product. This process enables quartz countertops to be extremely durable, nonabsorbent, and resistant to stains, scratches, and chips. Available in a wide range of designs, colors, and finishes, Cambria quartz surfaces also offer rich depth, texture, and versatile edge profiles for a truly luxurious look.

### 

What Is Quartzite?

Quartzite begins as a natural sandstone transformed by intense heat and pressure over time. This metamorphic process creates a durable stone with unique veining patterns, often resembling the look of marble. Blocks are then quarried, cut, shaped, and polished into slabs for use in design. Quartzite’s natural appearance and distinctiveness lend a timeless quality to interior spaces. However, due to its porous nature, it requires regular sealing to prevent staining and etching.

### 

Maintenance

Quartz and quartzite countertops have different maintenance needs due to their distinct compositions. Cambria quartz surfaces, being nonporous, are stain resistant, never need sealing, and are [NSF-51](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/quartz-is-the-ultimate-food-safe-surface) certified for safe food preparation. Simply wipe with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. To maintain its beauty, quartzite’s porous surface requires regular sealing and immediate cleanup of spills to prevent staining.

### 

Durability

Both quartz and quartzite are highly durable, known for their resistance to scratches and chips. This strength makes them excellent choices for high-traffic areas like kitchens. While quartzite is more heat resistant than quartz, we recommend using a closed-weave hot pad or solid trivet to protect either surface. ### 

Appearance

Quartzite countertops feature unique veining patterns, but their limited color options can restrict their use in different room aesthetics. Furthermore, the natural variation from slab to slab can make it challenging to match pieces within the same installation or space. Quartz countertops, on the other hand, offer a wide palette of consistent colors and patterns from slab to slab, ensuring a uniform look throughout your space. Due to its versatile nature across various styles and colors, quartz is a popular choice, with Cambria setting the standard for design innovation.
