## Before Your Cambria Installation

---

---

When my old countertop is removed, will I be without a sink?

Yes, but you can often arrange to have a [temporary sink](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/cooking-in-a-temporary-kitchen) installed. Ask your kitchen and bath dealer or your contractor for details.

Who will remove my old countertop?

Unless it is prearranged and included in the project contract, our installers will not tear out your existing countertop. It is your responsibility to arrange for the removal of your existing countertops prior to installation. All details should be worked out with your installer and detailed on your purchase agreement.

How can I prepare for my installation day?

Prepare for your new countertops by reviewing the [Cambria Installation Day Checklistopens in a new tab](https://cambriausa.com/content/dam/cusa/sales-marketing-collateral/sell-sheets/cambria-installation-checklist.pdf).

Who should I contact if I have questions about my countertop installation?

Your retailer or installation company will be happy to answer any questions you may have.

I've just purchased Cambria. What happens next?

Your installer will work with you to schedule appointments for measurements and installation. With new construction, your builder typically coordinates the installation for you, but confirm with them. If you have questions about your installation, reach out to your retailer or installation company. 

Is my Cambria slab going to look exactly like the samples I received?

The design sample is a small select cut from a slab; it does not fully exhibit all the design characteristics of the final installed product. We recommend visiting a local [gallery or slab showroom](https://cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/galleries-showrooms) to see a full-size slab.

How do I know my slab order status?

Your retailer or installation company will be happy to answer any questions you may have about your order status.

What paint colors would go well with my design?

Every Cambria design features a unique moodboard showcasing expertly matched paints and hardware inspiration. You can also utilize our online [paint match tool](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/planning/tools/paint-match) to get suggested Benjamin Moore paint — exclusively sold at locally owned paint, decorating, and hardware retailers.

How do I ensure an accurate measure or template of my countertop area?

All cabinets must be permanently installed and in the same plane. Also, sinks, faucet fixtures, and appliances should be available at the site during [final measurements](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-measure-your-kitchen-countertops). To verify cutout dimensions, you must review what is covered in your purchase agreement to ascertain responsibility of your installer.

How should I prepare walls or cabinets for installation of my new countertop?

To avoid installation difficulties, please be sure your walls and cabinets are structurally sound, straight, level, and square.

What kind of sink can I use?

Cambria can be used with any undermount or drop-in sink, including stainless steel, cast iron, porcelain, solid surface, or composite. [Farmhouse-style sinks](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/farmhouse-sinks-kitchen-countertops) can also be used.

Who is responsible for making sure all built-in elements (sink, stove, appliances, cabinets, faucet holes, etc.) fit together?

This is the responsibility of the homeowner, general contractor, or [Cambria retailer](https://cambriausa.com/dealer-locator/locations) and it should be detailed in your purchase agreement. If it is not, then the installer’s typical practices apply.

## During Your Cambria Installation

---

---

Will my walls and other adjacent surfaces be protected during installation?

Every effort is made to protect your walls and other adjacent surfaces. However, when a heavy [quartz countertop](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops) is installed, some scuffing to walls or adjacent fixtures may occur. Some “touch ups” may be necessary and are the responsibility of the homeowner.

Do fixtures and appliances need to be on-site during the installation?

Yes. For proper cutout size verification, it is necessary to have the sink and faucet fixtures as well as appliances available prior to installation.
