## Quartz vs. Marble

---

Curious about the similarities and differences of quartz and marble countertops? Explore what sets them apart.

---

### 

What Is Quartz?

Quartz countertops are made of natural quartz that is then combined with binders and pigments to create its final, beautiful product. This process enables quartz countertops to be maintenance free, extremely durable, nonabsorbent, and resistant to stains, scratches, and chips. Available in a wide range of designs, colors, and consistent patterns, quartz also presents a uniform look from slab to slab.

### 

What Is Marble?

Marble countertops are made from natural stone quarried in large blocks, then cut, shaped, and polished. Known for its sophisticated veining and unique patterns, each marble slab brings a one-of-a-kind luxury to any room. However, marble’s porous nature makes it more susceptible to staining, etching, and scratching, requiring regular care to maintain its beauty.

## Details That Matter

---

There’s a lot to consider when comparing quartz and marble. Here’s what to keep in mind.

---

---

### 

Maintenance

Quartz countertops are maintenance free, stain resistant, naturally nonabsorbent, and [NSF-51](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/quartz-is-the-ultimate-food-safe-surface) certified, and they never need sealing. To [clean](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-clean-quartz-countertops), just wipe with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. Because of its porous nature, marble requires ongoing care, including regular sealing to prevent staining from everyday substances like water, wine, and oil. If you’re willing to invest in its maintenance, marble can offer long-lasting beauty.

### 

Durability

Quartz is extremely durable, resisting chips, scratches, and stains. While beautiful, marble is softer, making it more prone to scratches and chips. Marble can handle heat better than quartz in some instances, but sudden temperature changes may cause damage to both, so the use of a trivet is always recommended. Cambria quartz surfaces are backed by a transferable [Full Lifetime Warranty](https://cambriausa.com/warranty) to provide peace of mind for a lifetime.

[Learn more about the durability of Cambria quartz countertops](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/are-quartz-countertops-durable)

---

### 

Appearance

Quartz countertops offer a wide range of consistent colors and patterns, ideal for any space aesthetic and ensuring a uniform look from slab to slab. Marble countertops feature unique veining patterns and colors. While a positive for some, this variation can make it challenging to match multiple slabs or find the right slab for your project. Marble typically is found in neutral colors, limiting options for those looking for darker or more bold countertop colors. Quartz is often preferred for its versatility within various styles and color schemes, with Cambria known as an industry leader in design innovation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

---

---

Do Cambria quartz countertops require care and maintenance?

No, keeping with its [care-free promise](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/product-care), Cambria requires no sealing, polishing, or reconditioning. Simply wipe with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. Due to its nonabsorbent nature, it does NOT require regular sealing and is stain resistant.

Is Cambria quartz surface a natural stone?

Cambria is made from pure natural quartz, pigments, and binders. Quartz is the extremely hard mineral that gives Cambria its strength. Our unique process results in a quartz product of elegance, style, and superior durability.

Do Cambria quartz surfaces stain?

Cambria’s nonporous and nonabsorbent surface is resistant to staining and eliminates the need for any sealing. Its nonabsorbent surface also greatly reduces the potential for bacterial growth from food absorbing into your countertop.
