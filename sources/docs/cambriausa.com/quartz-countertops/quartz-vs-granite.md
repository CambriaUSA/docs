## Quartz vs. Granite

---

Curious about the similarities and differences of quartz and granite countertops? Explore what sets them apart.

---

### 

What Is Quartz?

Quartz countertops are made of abundant natural quartz crystals that are then combined with binders and pigments to create its final, beautiful product. This process enables quartz countertops to be extremely durable, nonabsorbent, and resistant to stains, scratches, and chips. Available in a [wide range of designs, colors, and consistent patterns](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/top-ten-cambria-quartz-designs-granite-alternatives), quartz also presents a uniform look from slab to slab.

### 

What Is Granite?

Granite countertops are made from natural stone mined from quarries as large blocks later cut into smaller slabs. Each slab features unique patterns in a range of colors and designs. While granite is strong and durable, the process to create granite countertops leaves tiny spaces within the surface that can absorb liquids, potentially leading to staining if not regularly sealed.

## Details That Matter

---

There’s a lot to consider when comparing quartz and granite. Here’s what to keep in mind.

---

### 

Maintenance

Quartz countertops are maintenance free, stain resistant, naturally nonabsorbent, and [NSF-51](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/quartz-is-the-ultimate-food-safe-surface) certified, and they never need sealing. To clean, just wipe with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. While durable, granite countertops need to be sealed to maintain their beauty and prevent staining.

Durability

Since quartz is naturally harder than granite, it offers exceptional chip and scratch resistance. While granite countertops excel in heat resistance and impact durability, quartz’s resilience makes it ideal for daily use in high-traffic areas like kitchens and baths. ### 

Appearance

Quartz countertops offer a wide range of consistent colors and patterns, ideal for any designs and ensuring a uniform look. Granite countertops offer natural, unique variations that can pose more challenges when trying to find a slab that matches a kitchen or bath design. Quartz is often preferred for its [versatility](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/top-ten-cambria-quartz-designs-granite-alternatives) within various styles and color schemes, with Cambria known as an industry leader in design innovation.

---

How do Cambria quartz surfaces compare to granite?

Cambria is made from natural quartz, one of the hardest minerals on Earth, for superior durability and performance. With Cambria, you get the look and feel of granite, but with the benefit of it being nonabsorbent and maintenance free. Simply clean Cambria with warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. Cambria is stain resistant and never needs sealing or reconditioning. 

Is Cambria quartz surface a natural stone?

Cambria is made from pure natural quartz, pigments, and binders. Quartz is the extremely hard mineral that gives Cambria its strength. Our unique process results in a quartz product of elegance, style, and superior durability.
