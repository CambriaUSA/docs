Karpel Chair, U.S. International Trade Commission 500 E Street SW Washington, D.C. 20436 Re: Quartz Surface Products — Investigation No. TA-201-79 (Section 201 Global Safeguard Investigation) Dear Chair Karpel, We write regarding the Commission's ongoing Section 201 safeguard investigation under the Trade Act of 1974 concerning imports of quartz surface products. We appreciate the Commission's role in administering U.S. trade laws and conducting a thorough, fact-based review of whether increased imports are causing serious injury to a domestic industry. Cambria, a Minnesota-based manufacturer employing approximately 1,700 workers in Le Sueur, has raised concerns that a sustained surge of quartz surface imports has continued despite prior enforcement actions, including antidumping and countervailing duty orders imposed in 2019. According to the company, imports have shifted to other countries and through alleged transshipment, and traditional enforcement tools have not provided meaningful or durable relief. The Section 201 process provides an important mechanism for the Commission to evaluate whether a surge of imports—regardless of source—is a substantial cause of serious injury and, if so, to recommend appropriate, temporary relief. We respectfully express our support for the Commission's careful consideration of the record in this investigation and for the Section 201 process. We recognize that any determination must be grounded in the evidence developed by the Commission, and we do not prejudge the outcome. Given the potential implications for manufacturing jobs and domestic production capacity in Minnesota and across the country, we appreciate the Commission's attention to this matter and its continued commitment to ensuring U.S. trade laws are administered as Congress intended. Thank you for your consideration.
