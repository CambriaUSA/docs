### Key Takeaways

---

- Of all the popular countertop material options, quartz is the most stain-resistant option thanks to its nonporous, antimicrobial nature.
- Kitchens and bathrooms are spaces where stain-causing items are common, making quartz countertops the perfect choice for both spaces.
- Quartz countertops are perfect for busy lifestyles, thanks to how easy they are to clean; all you need is a soft cotton cloth, warm water, and mild soap, if desired.

---

Choosing a countertop material is an exciting part of the design process. Everyone wants their countertops to be beautiful because they’re either a visual focal point or an integral throughline that unifies a space. But, homeowners sometimes shy away from certain countertop materials or colors for fear of staining. The reality is that kitchens and bathrooms are high-traffic environments where all kinds of spills and splashes occur. And while many countertop materials may look beautiful in pictures, real life can tell another story. Because when common culprits like wine, coffee, or even a simple lemon wedge, can stain or etch your countertops, you may never enjoy your space as much as you had hoped. Quartz countertops are often preferred for both beauty and durability. 

---

## What Makes Quartz Different

Quartz countertops are made of abundant natural quartz crystals that are combined with binders and pigments to create its final, beautiful product. This process enables quartz countertops to be extremely [durable](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/are-quartz-countertops-durable), [nonabsorbent](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/quartz-is-the-ultimate-food-safe-surface), and [resistant to stains, scratches, and chips](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/maintenance-free-countertops-are-a-peak-kitchen-luxury).

Countertops made from stones like [granite](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/quartz-vs-granite) or [marble](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/quartz-vs-marble) are porous at a microscopic level and need to be regularly sealed to prevent the absorption of spills that can become stains. Maybe you’re excited about a particular stone because it’s beautiful and you don’t think you’ll mind sealing your countertops—but if you do it improperly or not as frequently as recommended, you may be at risk of future stains. 

[Cambria natural quartz surfaces](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops) don’t have any of those tiny capillaries to absorb liquids. So spills remain on the surface rather than penetrating—without ever needing to be sealed. And with Cambria’s wide range of designs, colors, and finishes, you can have the beautiful countertops you’ve been dreaming of—completely maintenance free.

## How to Clean Quartz Countertops

1. **Cleaning Quartz Surfaces Is Simple** 
 Harsh and heavy-duty cleaners are unnecessary with Cambria quartz surfaces. For easy cleanup, all you need to [clean your quartz countertops](https://cambriausa.com/cambria-style/blog/how-to-clean-quartz-countertops) is warm water, a soft cotton cloth, and mild soap, if desired. 
2. **No Need to Polish or Seal Quartz Surfaces** 
 Cambria quartz countertops never need routine polishing or sealing, which means countertop maintenance can be crossed off your to-do list for good.
3. **Note This Helpful Quartz Countertop Reminder** 
 Entertaining can be stressful, especially when cooking is involved. Your easy-to-clean quartz countertop should be the least of your worries. As with all stone surfaces, you should not put direct heat on Cambria, so be sure to keep a trivet or closed-weave hot pad handy to protect your countertops.

## 3 Things to Consider When Choosing Countertops

1. **Lifestyle** 
 Kitchens and bathrooms are highly visible spaces in a home. That’s probably why we spend countless hours dreaming of renovating our kitchens and bathrooms. We want them to be a reflection of our personal style. But, we also need them to be functional, especially for home cooks or entertainers. Heavy use and the presence of kids or pets are the types of lifestyle factors that call for durable, stain-resistant countertops designed to withstand daily wear.
2. **Maintenance Expectations** 
 Be honest with yourself about how willing you are to add another chore to your maintenance routine. Natural stones like granite and marble require regular sealing to help prevent stains. Cambria natural quartz surfaces are [maintenance free](https://cambriausa.com/quartz-countertops/product-care) and stain resistant. 
3. **Performance Beyond Stain Resistance** 
 Permanently stained or etched countertops can be an eye-sore, a constant reminder of an unfortunate spill. And countertop scratches, although maybe less visible, can still be an unsightly irritation. Nonporous Cambria quartz surfaces are stain and scratch resistant. Plus, that same non-porosity that prevents spills from becoming stains also prevents the growth of mold, mildew, and other bacteria, making it a food-safe choice for your kitchen and a hygienic option for your bathrooms.
