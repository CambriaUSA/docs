Cambria is deeply committed to the health and safety of all workers in the stone fabrication and installation industry. We believe that safety is more than a priority — it is a mandate. Through the Cambria SafetyFirst program, we provide fabricators and installers with the awareness, tools, and resources needed to protect their teams from silica dust exposure and maintain full compliance with OSHA standards. No worker in any segment of the industry should be exposed to dangerous levels of respirable crystalline silica in the workplace.

## Understanding the Silica Hazard

<Warning>
  Crystalline silica dust is a serious occupational health hazard. When stone is cut, ground, drilled, or otherwise processed, fine silica particles can become airborne. Prolonged or repeated exposure to respirable crystalline silica can cause **silicosis** — an irreversible and potentially fatal lung disease — as well as lung cancer and other serious respiratory illnesses. This hazard is real, and it is entirely preventable with proper engineering controls and safe work practices.
</Warning>

Crystalline silica is naturally present in quartz, granite, and other stone materials. The U.S. Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) have long identified respirable crystalline silica as a significant health hazard for workers in the stone fabrication and installation industry.

## The Cambria SafetyFirst Program

Cambria's SafetyFirst program is built on four pillars:

1. **Awareness** — creating industry-wide awareness about the risks of silica dust exposure in stone fabrication and installation
2. **Resources** — providing practical tools, guidelines, and documentation that fabricators can act on immediately
3. **Safe practices** — promoting and training on proven engineering controls and work practices that eliminate silica exposure at the source
4. **Compliance support** — supporting fabricators in meeting OSHA silica standards and helping them build a culture of safety throughout their operations

## Safe Fabrication Practices

The following engineering controls and work practices form the foundation of a safe fabrication environment. These are not optional — they are OSHA requirements for shops working with silica-containing materials.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use Wet Cutting Methods">
    Always use wet cutting, grinding, and polishing to suppress silica dust at the source. Comprehensive wet processing is the single most effective engineering control for silica exposure in stone fabrication.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wear Proper Respiratory Protection">
    When wet cutting is not feasible or air monitoring indicates exposure above action levels, workers must wear NIOSH-approved respirators rated for silica (minimum N95, or a higher-rated respirator as required by monitoring results).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Maintain Proper Ventilation">
    Ensure your fabrication area has adequate ventilation to prevent accumulation of airborne particulates. Local exhaust ventilation (LEV) at point-of-generation is strongly recommended.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Conduct Regular Air Monitoring">
    Perform regular air monitoring in your shop to measure worker exposure to respirable crystalline silica. OSHA requires employers to assess exposure levels and implement controls accordingly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow OSHA Table 1 Engineering Controls">
    OSHA's Table 1 specifies required engineering and work practice controls for each task type. Review and implement Table 1 controls for all applicable fabrication operations at your shop.
  </Step>
</Steps>
