## Detailed Certification Profiles

### GREENGUARD Gold Certification

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Issuing Body" icon="building-2">
    UL Environment (Underwriters Laboratories)
  </Card>
  <Card title="Standard" icon="file-text">
    UL 2818 GREENGUARD Gold
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

GREENGUARD Gold is one of the most rigorous indoor air quality certifications available. To earn it, a product must demonstrate low volatile organic compound (VOC) chemical emissions through continuous, independent testing — the Gold tier imposes stricter limits than the base GREENGUARD standard, specifically to protect sensitive populations such as children and the elderly.

**What this means for you:**

- Your Cambria surface contributes to a healthier indoor environment from day one.
- GREENGUARD Gold certification allows Cambria to contribute points toward **LEED v4 Indoor Environmental Quality (IEQ)** credits — a key consideration for green building projects.
- No off-gassing of harmful chemicals into your home's air.

<Info>
  GREENGUARD Gold certification is particularly relevant for kitchens, nurseries, schools, and healthcare environments where indoor air quality directly affects health and well-being.
</Info>

### NSF International NSF-51 — Food Safety Certification

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Issuing Body" icon="building-2">
    NSF International
  </Card>
  <Card title="Standard" icon="file-text">
    NSF/ANSI 51 — Food Equipment Materials
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

NSF-51 is the gold standard for food contact surface safety. Administered by NSF International — a globally recognized public health and safety organization — this certification verifies that a material is safe for direct contact with food and is appropriate for use in food preparation and splash zones.

**What this means for you:**

- You can cut, prep, and work directly on your Cambria countertop with confidence that no harmful substances will leach into your food.
- Every Cambria surface passes NSF-51 testing requirements for food safety, cleanliness, and hygienic performance.
- Because Cambria is **nonporous and nonabsorbent**, bacteria, mold, and mildew have nowhere to take hold — reinforcing its suitability for food preparation environments.

<Tip>
  NSF-51 certification applies across all Cambria designs and finishes, not just selected product lines. Every surface you choose is food-contact safe.
</Tip>

### Health Product Declaration (HPD) v2.1

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Issuing Body" icon="building-2">
    Health Product Declaration Collaborative
  </Card>
  <Card title="Standard" icon="file-text">
    HPD Open Standard v2.1
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

A Health Product Declaration is a standardized disclosure document that lists all the ingredients in a building product — including chemical components, their hazard classifications, and their concentrations. HPD v2.1 is the current open standard widely required by architects, specifiers, and project teams pursuing LEED, WELL, and Living Building Challenge credits.

**What this means for you:**

- You have full visibility into what your Cambria surface is made of, with no proprietary "black box" chemistry.
- Design professionals and sustainability consultants can reference Cambria's HPD to satisfy project documentation requirements for green building certifications.
- The HPD listing supports projects centered on **materials transparency, occupant wellness, and environmental responsibility**.

### Declare Label — Living Building Challenge Compliant

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Issuing Body" icon="building-2">
    International Living Future Institute (ILFI)
  </Card>
  <Card title="Standard" icon="file-text">
    Living Building Challenge (LBC) Declare Label
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The Declare label is often described as a "nutrition label" for building products. Issued by the International Living Future Institute, it discloses a product's ingredients, its compliance status with the LBC Red List, and its country of origin. A Declare listing is a prerequisite for many projects pursuing Living Building Challenge certification — the most rigorous green building standard in existence.

**What this means for you:**

- Cambria's Declare label confirms that its products contain **no Red List chemicals** — the LBC's list of the worst-in-class materials known to harm human and ecosystem health.
- Cambria is verified free of **EPA priority chemicals, COC (Chemicals of Concern), and REACH-listed substances**.
- Specifiers, architects, and green building professionals can use Cambria's Declare listing directly as project documentation.

<Note>
  The Declare database entry for Cambria quartz is publicly searchable at [declare.living-future.org](https://declare.living-future.org/products/cambria-quartz), making verification simple for any project team.
</Note>

### Living Building Challenge (LBC) Compliant

The Living Building Challenge is the world's most rigorous and holistic green building standard, administered by the International Living Future Institute. LBC compliance means a building product has been evaluated against the most demanding criteria for human health, environmental impact, and chemical transparency.

**What this means for you:**

- Projects pursuing LBC certification — the pinnacle of sustainable construction — can specify Cambria surfaces as a compliant product.
- LBC compliance reinforces everything represented by Cambria's Declare label: full ingredient disclosure, no harmful chemicals, and verified environmental responsibility.

### Build America, Buy America Act (BABA) Compliant

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Standard" icon="flag">

The Build America, Buy America Act requires that products used in federally funded infrastructure projects be manufactured in the United States. **What this means for you:**

- Government agencies, municipalities, schools, hospitals, and contractors working on federally funded projects can specify Cambria without any compliance concerns.
- BABA compliance is not a certification Cambria has applied for — it is a natural consequence of the fact that every Cambria slab is designed, produced, and finished in the USA.
- Combined with Cambria's domestic supply chain and 30+ US distribution centers, BABA compliance means faster, simpler procurement for public projects.
